# The Falconer's Handbook — Business & Monetisation Plan

> This is a planning document, not code. No paywall, account system, or
> payment integration has been built. This document proposes what to
> build, in what order, at what price, and why.
> Every number in this document should be treated as a **reasoned starting
> estimate** to react to, not a forecast.

---

## 1. The product

**The Falconer's Handbook** — a bilingual (Arabic-first, English second),
citation-backed, digital falconry guide. Currently ships with eight
chapters covering Overview, Nutrition, Health & Prevention, Supplements &
Vitamins, Daily Care, Housing & Environment, Common Problems, and
Training. Four more chapters planned (Breeds & Types, Breeding, History &
Heritage, A–Z Glossary).

Every health, nutrition, and medical claim is cited from published
veterinary sources (Merck Veterinary Manual, Neil Forbes / Honeybrook
Farm, Souq Waqif Falcon Hospital, The Modern Apprentice, IAF). The
product has a distinct design language (cream editorial palette, warm
typography, dark-mode support), first-class Arabic typography, and a
falcon-species selector throughout that adapts content between saker,
peregrine, and gyrfalcon.

## 2. Market

### Segmentation

- **Serious working falconers worldwide** — an estimated 50,000 to
  100,000 people. Highly engaged, professional or semi-professional,
  willing to pay for tools that save them time and keep birds alive.
  Geographically concentrated in the Gulf, the UK, the US, continental
  Europe, and Japan.
- **Hobbyist and apprentice falconers** — larger, perhaps 200,000–500,000
  globally, at various stages of learning. Price-sensitive but
  reliability-sensitive too. Good free-tier conversion target.
- **Adjacent audiences** — veterinary students specialising in avian
  medicine, bird-of-prey keepers outside of working falconry, regional
  associations, zoos and conservation centres. Smaller but buy in groups.

### Geography

- **Gulf (UAE, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, Kuwait, Bahrain, Oman)** — the
  single strongest market. Falconry is cultural heritage, state-funded
  research, and daily practice here. Arabic-first content is a defensible
  wedge almost nothing else offers.
- **UK and US** — mature hobbyist markets with established clubs and
  publications. English-only is fine for these.
- **Continental Europe** — similar, smaller scale.
- **Japan** — small, growing interest, would need a third locale later.

### Competition

| Product | Strength | Gap |
|---|---|---|
| **The Modern Apprentice** (website) | Free, deep, community-vetted | Dated UX, English-only, static, no interactive tools |
| **Nick Fox — *Understanding the Bird of Prey*** | Authoritative reference | Physical book, expensive (~£70+), no interactive features, out of reach for many new falconers |
| **Vetafarm / supplement companies** | Product expertise | Product-centric marketing, not teaching |
| **Gulf falcon hospitals** (Souq Waqif, Abu Dhabi) | Clinical authority | Medical services, not learning content |
| **Falconry clubs / IAF** | Credibility, network | Membership-based, not content products |

The Falconer's Handbook fits a specific gap: **bilingual, digital,
teaching-centric, species-differentiated, and eventually interactive**
(see roadmap). No competitor currently occupies that corner of the
market.

## 3. Positioning

**Tagline candidates**:
- "The falconry guide you'll actually use."
- "Nutrition, health, training — for sakers, peregrines, and gyrfalcons."
- "دليل الصقار — دليل رقمي ثنائي اللغة لصقارة جادة."

**What the product *is*:**
- A structured, cited, bilingual learning product for falconry
- A daily reference that earns its place in the falconer's routine
- Eventually, a falcon-management tool (moult tracker, logbooks)

**What the product *is not*:**
- A replacement for in-person mentorship (it says this explicitly)
- A replacement for a licensed avian vet (it says this repeatedly)
- A veterinary-prescription tool (the pharmaceutical tab carries a
  non-dismissable warning)

**Defensible moats**:
- **Bilingual Arabic-first** — very few competitors ship serious
  Arabic-language falconry content. Translation is the easy part;
  understanding Gulf falconry tradition enough to write natively is
  the hard part.
- **Species-differentiated content** — the saker / peregrine /
  gyrfalcon tab pattern appears throughout and would be expensive for
  a competitor to replicate credibly.
- **Citation discipline** — every medical claim traces to a named
  published source. Content that looks AI-generated without citations
  is already the internet's worst kind; being provably the opposite is
  a real moat.

## 4. Freemium tier design

### Free tier

Shown to every visitor, no account required:
- **Overview** (full)
- **History & Heritage** (full, when written — strong SEO piece)
- **A–Z Glossary** (full, when written — strong SEO piece)
- **First ~25% of each major chapter** as a preview, then a paywall card
  prompting sign-up
- A "pick your falcon" species selector preview on the home page

Rationale: the free tier has to be generous enough for casuals to share
and link to, and for search engines to index richly, but not so generous
that serious falconers never pay. History and Glossary are chosen for
free status because they rank well for discovery queries ("what is a
cadge", "falconry history") and are low-conversion on their own.

### Premium tier

Behind a single $12/month subscription, or $96/year:
- Every full chapter, including species-specific content behind each tab
  (the saker / peregrine / gyrfalcon variants)
- Downloadable PDF/printable versions of each chapter
- Eventually: the moult tracker, logbooks, and interactive tools (see
  roadmap)

### Why a simple two-tier rather than three tiers

Three tiers (Free / Pro / Vet) sounds good on paper but adds paywall
decision friction without materially growing revenue at this market
size. A future "Vet / Professional" tier makes sense only once a
partner vet relationship or clinic integration is in place — add it in
year 2.

## 5. Pricing

### Recommended primary pricing

- **$12 / month**
- **$96 / year** (saves $48 — about 33%)
- **$249 one-time** lifetime access (founding-member, limited)

### Rationale

Comparable niche paid content:
- Specialist newsletters and learning products: $8–$15/mo typical
- Hobbyist-professional digital reference: $10–$20/mo
- Falconry specifically: no direct digital-subscription comparable — Nick
  Fox's book sits at ~£70 physical, suggesting willingness to pay

Falconry's audience skews **serious hobbyist and professional**.
Per-user willingness to pay is higher than typical consumer SaaS.
$12/month is a reasoned mid-point; the annual price (save 33%) will be
the default selection.

### Gulf pricing note

Consider launching at **$14.99 / month** in the Gulf initially. Arabic
differentiation + higher willingness to pay + first-mover advantage.
Easier to lower than raise later. Check local payment processor fees
and currency conversions — net take-home per subscriber may be closer
between markets than the sticker price suggests.

### Lifetime pricing

$249 one-time for **founding members** (first 100 subscribers, capped
and visible). Creates urgency, seeds early revenue, builds a committed
cohort who give feedback and generate word-of-mouth. Remove the lifetime
option once capped; don't reopen it, because it cannibalises subscription
revenue permanently.

## 6. Revenue projections — niche-realistic

These are **reasoned starting estimates** for a niche market, not
aspirational SaaS forecasts. Falconry's audience is finite; penetration
into 1% of the global serious-falconer population would be a strong
outcome.

| Year | Subscribers | Avg $/mo | ARR (USD) | Context |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1** (soft launch, content only) | 150–300 | $10 | **$18,000–$36,000** | Gulf + early English audience, word-of-mouth, no paid ads |
| **2** (moult tracker + first logbooks) | 500–1,000 | $11 | **$66,000–$132,000** | Interactive tools drive retention, premium features justify pricing, some paid growth |
| **3** (mature product, established in Gulf) | 1,500–3,000 | $11.50 | **$207,000–$414,000** | Established brand, clinic partnerships, ask-a-vet tier possible |

Costs to hold against this: hosting (trivial — Vercel is free/cheap at
this scale), content (user's own time, or a translator if scaling),
payment processing (~2.9% + $0.30 per transaction via Stripe), any paid
marketing (conservative assumption: reinvest ~15% of year-1 revenue),
any vet consultation partner revenue share (year 2+).

**What would change these numbers dramatically:**
- A Gulf-ruler endorsement or a major falcon hospital partnership —
  could 5× year-1 alone
- A single viral piece of content on Gulf social media — network
  effects matter in tight communities
- Competitor emergence — nothing currently occupies this space, but
  that could change; defensible moats (Arabic depth, citation
  discipline, interactive tools) matter more in year 2+
- Product quality slip — one public case of inaccurate medical advice
  would permanently damage trust in a community this tight

## 7. Anti-screenshot and retention

The user flagged screenshots as the monetisation risk. A few static
chapters can be screenshotted and shared; a whole product can't. The
strategy is to make the product **do things for the user** that are
expensive to recreate from a screenshot.

### Strategy 1: Personalisation

The more the app tracks the **specific bird**, the less screenshotable
it becomes. This is the core logic behind the moult tracker (requested
by the user):

- User logs when their saker enters the moulting chamber
- App predicts the moult duration based on species, age, size
- Shows a 3D feather model rendering progress across weeks
- Sends reminders when mid-moult check-ins are due
- Logs feather drops, records any issues, correlates with past moults

A screenshot of the moult tracker for someone else's bird helps
nobody. The value is in the continuous personal record.

### Strategy 2: Interactive tools > static content

Content that can't be captured in a single image:

- **Weight tracker** with anomaly detection and trend alerts
- **Moult progress tracker** with the 3D feather model (above)
- **Feeding log** with portion tracking and cost summaries
- **Training log** with session notes and progression graphs
- **Symptom triage** — interactive version of the Health chapter's
  symptom table that walks the user through diagnosis questions and
  outputs a vet-call / monitor / home-care recommendation
- **Vet-call decision helper** — the existing "when to call the vet"
  blocks from each chapter, interactive and bird-specific

### Strategy 3: Progressive unlock in context

Premium content surfaces when it is most relevant:

- User logs a weight drop → the Health chapter's weight-drop triage
  surfaces immediately, paywall-free if premium
- User logs the bird entering the chamber → moult-related chapters
  and the moult tracker become the default view
- User logs a training session → training chapter excerpts offered as
  related reading

Screenshot of that surfaced content is useful only for the moment it
applies; a non-subscriber doesn't get the surfacing, only the raw
content.

### Strategy 4: Community and Q&A

- Member-only community forum
- Periodic "ask a falconer" or vet-partner Q&A (moderated)
- Seasonal live events (pre-hunting-season Q&A, pre-moult Q&A)

None of this is screenshot-transferable because it's conversational
and time-bound.

### Strategy 5: Fresh-content cadence

- Monthly new article or seasonal guide
- Member-only updates to existing chapters (the user sees
  "added 2026-04-19" badges)
- Archive stays behind the paywall; screenshots of year-old content
  aren't the current product

### Strategy 6: Video

Video of technique (lure swing, creance progression, hood acclimation,
hooding close-up, casting recognition) is difficult to screenshot
usefully. A diagram can be copied; a 90-second demonstration can't.
Video production is work but a few well-made clips anchor the premium
tier meaningfully.

### Strategy 7: Structured courses and certificates

- "Falconry Essentials" — a structured 20-lesson course that unlocks
  week by week, with completion tracking
- Certificate of completion (digital, with member name and date)
- The user pays for **the process**, not the raw content

### Strategy 8: Watermarking on PDFs (light)

If we offer printable PDF downloads of chapters, watermark with the
subscriber's email in the footer. Not intrusive, doesn't harm legitimate
use, but discourages PDF sharing on public forums.

## 8. Product roadmap

Where the moult tracker and logbook fit in the overall build order.

### Phase C (current) — content and brand
- **Status: shipping**
- Eight chapters live, four remaining
- Bilingual, citation-backed, species-differentiated
- Design language and dark mode stable
- Pivoted to falconry-only site structure

### Phase D — accounts and single-bird profile
- Email + password auth, or Magic Link
- Per-user account with one or more **falcon profiles** (name,
  species, hatch date, notes, photo upload)
- Manual **flying weight logbook** — daily weight entries, trend graph,
  5–10% drop alert rule lifted from the Health chapter
- **This is the phase where the paywall first exists**. Premium unlocks
  the gated chapters plus the logbook.
- Stripe for payments. Annual option from day one.

### Phase E — the moult tracker (user-requested flagship)
- Per-falcon **moult event**: entry date, exit date (predicted and
  actual), notes per day
- **Moult prediction** based on species, age, hatch season, climate
  (Gulf summer accelerates certain stages, delays others)
- **3D feather progression model** — a rendered falcon showing which
  feather groups have moulted, which are in progress, which remain.
  Updates as the user logs feather drops
- Daily check-in reminders during moult season
- Historical view — previous years' moults for the same bird,
  comparison across birds

This feature alone may justify the premium tier's existence for many
users. The moult is central to the falconer's calendar and no one
else offers this.

### Phase F — broader logbooks
- **Training log** — per-session notes, weight, weather, duration,
  what was practised, how it went, species-specific progression view
- **Feeding log** — what, when, how much, from which supplier (cost
  tracking for pros)
- **Vet-visit log** — appointments, prescriptions, follow-ups, records
  to produce for a future vet visit
- **Equipment log** — when each piece was acquired, condition, when
  it's due for replacement (jesses especially)

### Phase G — interactive symptom triage
- The Health chapter's symptom table, but interactive
- Walks the user through a decision tree (stance, breathing, crop,
  droppings, weight trend from their logbook)
- Outputs a vet-call / monitor / home-care recommendation
- Reads the user's flying weight log for the "sudden drop > 10%" check
- Integrates with Common Problems chapter content for the "what to
  try first" recommendation

### Phase H — community and ask-a-vet
- Member-only forum (probably on a hosted platform like Discourse with
  the site as primary presence)
- Monthly live Q&A with a partner vet (initially free for subscribers,
  later a higher-tier add-on)
- Photo sharing / falcon showcase (opt-in, moderated)

### Longer term
- Third locale (Japanese, French?)
- Live weight-monitor integrations (smart-scale hardware) — ambitious
- Breeder-centric tools (egg logbook, hatch timeline)
- Marketplace adjacencies — don't pursue unless it clearly fits

## 9. Launch strategy

### Pre-launch (current)

- Site is live on Vercel at `animal-farming.vercel.app` (will become
  a custom domain before launch — suggested: `falconer.app`,
  `falconers-handbook.com`, or an Arabic variant)
- Eight chapters complete, four remaining
- No paywall yet
- Social presence: nil — to build

### Soft launch (Phase D ships)

- Target: Gulf falconry community first
- Waitlist already active during Phase D build
- First 100 subscribers get the $249 lifetime price
- Early-adopter Discord or community channel
- Content marketing: publish History & Heritage and Glossary as free
  public SEO pieces with `sitemap.xml` discoverability (already shipped
  in Phase C.4)
- Partner outreach: one Gulf falcon hospital for content endorsement
  (offer free subscriptions to their staff — credibility exchange)

### Public launch (Phase E ships with moult tracker)

- "We have the moult tracker" is the launch headline
- Paid social on platforms falconers actually use — Instagram for Gulf,
  Twitter/X for UK/US, regional Gulf channels
- Falconry-club outreach: IAF, UK Hawk Board, NAFA, regional Gulf
  associations
- Influencer (reasonable falconer YouTubers and Instagram accounts) —
  free subscription + honest review, not sponsored content
- Conference attendance or sponsorship: the International Festival of
  Falconry, Abu Dhabi International Hunting and Equestrian Exhibition
  ("ADIHEX")

### Growth mode (year 2)

- Ongoing content cadence (monthly new article, seasonal guides)
- Clinic partnerships (discounted subscriptions bundled with vet
  services)
- Referral programme — free month for each successful referral
- Expand logbook features based on which get most usage in year 1

## 10. What could go wrong

- **Medical inaccuracy** — one high-visibility case of content
  recommending something that harmed a bird would permanently damage
  trust. Mitigation: citation discipline, licensed vet review before
  any new medical claim, clear disclaimers already in place.
- **Gulf regulatory changes** — falconry and quarry laws change; need
  to keep Gulf-specific content current (hunting quarry, transport,
  import/export).
- **Competitor emergence** — nothing occupies this corner today but a
  well-resourced entrant (a falcon hospital building their own product,
  a book author going digital) could change that. Mitigation: the
  existing moats (bilingual, species-differentiated, citation) are
  defensible if maintained.
- **User-acquisition cost** — falconry is small enough that paid
  advertising has limited reach; growth will be slow and
  word-of-mouth-driven. Mitigation: lean into the niche, don't
  burn cash on broad ads.
- **Content maintenance burden** — medical literature updates, new
  product availability, regulatory change all require ongoing content
  work. Budget this as a recurring cost.
- **Technical debt** — the app is in good shape today but adding
  accounts, payments, logbooks, and 3D rendering will expand the
  codebase significantly. Plan to onboard a second developer before
  Phase F.

## 11. Decisions to make before starting Phase D

1. **Legal entity and jurisdiction** — where is the business
   registered? Tax implications differ a lot between UAE, UK, US,
   Ireland, etc. Payment processing availability varies.
2. **Domain name** — buy the good ones now before the product is
   visible enough to attract squatters. Suggestions: `falconer.app`,
   `thefalconershandbook.com`, an Arabic variant.
3. **Brand / logo** — the current logo is a generic placeholder. A
   proper identity (logo, colour extension, typography hierarchy)
   matters before launch — probably worth commissioning.
4. **Vet partner** — who does the "consult a vet" line point to?
   A named partner hospital gives credibility and is a referral
   source. Worth securing before launch.
5. **Content localisation strategy for year 2** — if Japanese is the
   next locale, who translates? Same questions for French.
6. **Pricing experiments** — whether to launch with a limited-time
   founding-member price or with normal pricing. Founding is
   recommended for urgency but commits you to a public price after.
7. **The moult tracker's depth** — how much effort goes into the 3D
   feather model vs. a simpler progress bar? A polished 3D model is
   the user's vision; a clean progress bar ships in a third of the
   time and captures 70% of the value. Recommend simpler first,
   upgrade later.
