This glossary is the index to the other eleven chapters. Every specialised term used across **Overview**, **History & Heritage**, **Breeds & Types**, **Housing & Environment**, **Nutrition**, **Daily Care**, **Training**, **Health & Prevention**, **Supplements & Vitamins**, **Common Problems**, and **Breeding** is listed here with a short definition and a link back to the chapter where the term gets its full treatment.

Scope: terms are drawn from actual usage in those chapters, not from a comprehensive falconry lexicon. Arabic-specific terminology (الحرّ, الشاهين, الجير, الصقّار, المقيض, غرفة الطيور) is preserved in both locale files. Species binomials (*Falco cherrug*, *Falco peregrinus*, *Falco rusticolus*) and other Latin terms are italicised throughout.

## A

**Abbasid Caliphate** — 8th–13th c. AD; the Islamic caliphate during which falconry was firmly established as a sport of Arab rulers and referenced in the Arabic literary record. *See [History & Heritage](/en/guide/history).*

**Abu Dhabi Falcon Hospital** — Established 1999; one of the largest falcon-specific veterinary facilities in the world, anchoring the UAE's modern falcon health infrastructure. *See [History & Heritage](/en/guide/history).*

**Air cell** — The air space at the blunt end of an incubated egg; grows steadily through incubation as water evaporates through the shell. Tracked via candling and egg-weight monitoring to calibrate incubator humidity. *See [Breeding](/en/guide/breeding).*

**Al-Hurr (الحرّ)** — The Arabic name for the saker falcon, literally "the free one". Carries the Bedouin understanding of the bird as a seasonal partner rather than a permanent possession. *See [History & Heritage](/en/guide/history).*

**Al-Jeer (الجير)** — The Arabic name for the gyrfalcon, "the northern visitor". *See [Breeds & Types](/en/guide/breeds).*

**Al-Saqqar (الصقّار)** — The Arabic word for falconer. *See [History & Heritage](/en/guide/history).*

**Al-Shaheen (الشاهين)** — The Arabic name for the peregrine falcon, carrying pre-Islamic poetic resonance. Historically applied to the *calidus* subspecies wintering in the Gulf. *See [Breeds & Types](/en/guide/breeds).*

**Altai falcon / Altai morph** — A darker, heavily-spotted colour morph within *Falco cherrug milvipes*, not a separate species or hybrid. Confirmed as a saker morph (not a distinct taxon) by a 2023 genetic study. *See [Breeds & Types](/en/guide/breeds).*

**Altai Mountains** — The Central Asian mountain range where rock etchings (circa 1000 BC) show hunters with birds of prey, one of the earliest visual records of falconry. *See [History & Heritage](/en/guide/history).*

***anatum*** — *Falco peregrinus anatum*, the American "duck hawk" subspecies of peregrine from the Rocky Mountains and beyond; historically the peregrine of United States falconry. *See [Breeds & Types](/en/guide/breeds).*

**Artificial insemination (AI)** — Depositing semen into the female via fine-tip syringe during her receptive window; cloacal (vaginal) insemination is standard, surgical AI into the oviduct is a specialist alternative. Timed 3–5 days before the first egg and continued through the laying window. *See [Breeding](/en/guide/breeding).*

**Aspergillosis** — The most serious and most common cause of death in captive Gulf falcons; a fungal infection of the lungs and air sacs caused by inhaling *Aspergillus fumigatus* spores. Prevention is through clean dry housing, clean food, and humidity control rather than treatment. *See [Health & Prevention](/en/guide/health).*

***Aspergillus fumigatus*** — The fungal species causing aspergillosis. Its spores germinate in warm, humid conditions; prevention centres on keeping housing dry rather than simply cool. *See [Housing & Environment](/en/guide/housing).*

**Aspiration pneumonia** — Food or fluid entering the windpipe during feeding, almost always fatal in chicks. Caused by force-feeding on the back, feeding too fast, or pieces that are too large. *See [Breeding](/en/guide/breeding).*

**Assisted hatching** — Carefully helping a chick unzip when it has made an external pip but has not progressed after 24 hours. Specialist intervention only; helping too early is commonly fatal. *See [Breeding](/en/guide/breeding).*

**Astroturf** — The standard padded covering for perches: soft enough for daily standing, textured enough to discourage slipping, cleanable, and raptor-appropriate. *See [Housing & Environment](/en/guide/housing).*

**Avian pox** — A viral disease producing pox-like dry-crust lesions on the cere, eyelids, or unfeathered skin. Vaccination is available in some regions. *See [Health & Prevention](/en/guide/health).*

## B

***babylonicus*** — *Falco peregrinus babylonicus*, the red-naped shaheen; a smaller, more saker-like peregrine subspecies from eastern Iran through the Hindu Kush and Tian Shan. *See [Breeds & Types](/en/guide/breeds).*

**Backcross** — A mating between an F1 hybrid and one of the original parent species; fertility becomes more variable than F1 × F1. *See [Breeding](/en/guide/breeding).*

**Barring** — The dark horizontal bars across the lower belly of peregrines; clean and regular barring indicates healthy juvenile development. *See [Breeds & Types](/en/guide/breeds).*

**Bating** — When a bird jumps from its perch in panic or stress, often flapping into the leash. Indicates poor manning, environmental stress, fear, or pain. *See [Daily Care](/en/guide/daily-care).*

**Bedouin seasonal cycle** — The traditional Arab falconry pattern: capture migrating passage falcons (sakers, shaheens) in autumn, fly them through winter on desert quarry (houbara, stone curlew, hare), and release them back to the wild in spring. The falcon as a seasonal partner, not a permanent possession. *See [History & Heritage](/en/guide/history).*

**Bewit** — The technique of fastening leather jesses to the bird's legs. *See [Daily Care](/en/guide/daily-care).*

**Bind** — To strike and lock talons onto quarry in flight; a successful capture. *See [Training](/en/guide/training).*

**Biosecurity** — The hygiene practices that prevent disease transmission between birds: separate daily rooms preferred, physical separation mandatory if sharing, hand washing between birds, equipment disinfection, separate cleaning tools per room. *See [Housing & Environment](/en/guide/housing).*

**Block perch** — The classic fixed perch: a padded top (typically Astroturf) on a low stable base. Used for most of the day; diameter and padding are critical for preventing bumblefoot. *See [Housing & Environment](/en/guide/housing).*

**Blood feather** — A growing feather still vascular during moult; if broken, will bleed until removed or the shaft seals. *See [Common Problems](/en/guide/problems).*

***Boke of Saint Albans*** — A 15th-century English text that formalised the medieval European "hawk-of-station" tables assigning specific falcons to specific social ranks. More literary than operational. *See [History & Heritage](/en/guide/history).*

**Bow perch** — A curved perching bar less common for falcons than for hawks. *See [Housing & Environment](/en/guide/housing).*

**Brancher** — A bird taken from near the nest just after fledging, when young are still on nearby branches. Intermediate in wildness between an eyass and a passage bird. *See [Breeds & Types](/en/guide/breeds).*

**Brooder** — The small enclosed heated unit, lined with soft clean cloth, that holds hand-raised chicks. Temperature starts at 35–37 °C and reduces by roughly 1 °C per week. *See [Breeding](/en/guide/breeding).*

***brookei*** — *Falco peregrinus brookei*, the Mediterranean peregrine (sometimes called the "Maltese falcon"). *See [Breeds & Types](/en/guide/breeds).*

**Bumblefoot** — Inflammation and infection of the foot pad; multifactorial causes include hard perches, overweight, wet perches, punctures, and unilateral weight-bearing. Progresses from smooth patch to pink swelling to scab to ulcerated lesion. Also called pododermatitis. *See [Health & Prevention](/en/guide/health).*

## C

**Cadge** — A portable padded perching frame used to transport multiple birds over distance or briefly hold several birds in a staging area. Not a long-term housing solution. *See [Housing & Environment](/en/guide/housing).*

**Cade & Weaver** — Editors of the Peregrine Fund's *Falcon Propagation: A Manual on Captive Breeding*; the canonical reference for captive falcon breeding worldwide. *See [Breeding](/en/guide/breeding).*

**Calcium gluconate** — Injectable calcium for emergency hypocalcaemia, especially egg-binding seizures; a vet-administered emergency drug. *See [Supplements & Vitamins](/en/guide/supplements).*

***calidus*** — *Falco peregrinus calidus*, the tundra peregrine; breeds in high-Arctic Russia and Scandinavia, winters in the Middle East, East Africa, and South Asia. Historically the peregrine encountered by Gulf falconers in passage. *See [Breeds & Types](/en/guide/breeds).*

**Candling** — Using a strong focused light to backlight an egg and observe embryo development. Standard candling is at 7, 14, and 21 days. *See [Breeding](/en/guide/breeding).*

**Captive-bred** — Hatched in a registered breeding facility from known parents, with full documentation. The modern ethical and legal standard worldwide. *See [Breeds & Types](/en/guide/breeds).*

**Carnidazole (Spartrix)** — The drug of choice for frounce (trichomoniasis); highly effective if caught early. *See [Supplements & Vitamins](/en/guide/supplements).*

**Cast / Casting** — The dry pellet of indigestible material (fur, feathers, small bone fragments) that a falcon regurgitates 12–24 hours after a meal containing roughage. Passing a clean firm cast on time is one of the best daily health indicators. *See [Daily Care](/en/guide/daily-care).*

**Cere** — The bare skin around the nostrils at the base of the beak. Colour changes with age (yellow in adults, blue-grey in juveniles) and with health. *See [Breeds & Types](/en/guide/breeds).*

**Chamber-raised adult** — A breeding bird raised naturally by its parents with minimal human contact; the input stock for natural-pair breeding, as opposed to imprint birds. *See [Breeding](/en/guide/breeding).*

**Chelation therapy** — The veterinary emergency treatment for lead poisoning; drugs bind the lead and enable excretion. *See [Health & Prevention](/en/guide/health).*

***cherrug*** — *Falco cherrug cherrug*, the nominate "steppe saker" subspecies, ranging from central Europe through Kazakhstan. *See [Breeds & Types](/en/guide/breeds).*

**CITES** — The Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species; the international treaty regulating movement of protected species. Gyrfalcons and peregrines are commonly Appendix I (strictest), sakers commonly Appendix II. Captive-bred certificates are required for legal international movement. *See [Breeding](/en/guide/breeding).*

**Climate control** — The combination of air conditioning, humidity control, evaporative cooling, shade, and airflow essential for Gulf falconry. Non-negotiable for serious keeping, especially of gyrfalcons. *See [Housing & Environment](/en/guide/housing).*

**Closed ring** — A permanent leg ring fitted to a chick while the tarsus is still small enough to accept it (typically day 10–14). Required for captive-bred certification. *See [Breeding](/en/guide/breeding).*

**Cloacal prolapse** — Protrusion of the cloaca after straining during laying or heavy incubation; a vet emergency requiring prompt reduction. *See [Breeding](/en/guide/breeding).*

**Clutch** — The complete set of eggs laid in one breeding cycle: saker typically 3–5, peregrine around 4, gyrfalcon 2–4. *See [Breeding](/en/guide/breeding).*

***coatsi*** — *Falco cherrug coatsi*, the "desert saker" subspecies of Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. *See [Breeds & Types](/en/guide/breeds).*

**Coccidiosis** — A protozoan intestinal infection common in young captive-bred falcons; causes watery green diarrhoea, weight loss, poor condition. Treated with toltrazuril or sulphonamide drugs. *See [Health & Prevention](/en/guide/health).*

**Coping** — Precise trimming of an overgrown beak; a vet procedure, as live tissue and vasculature are at risk. *See [Common Problems](/en/guide/problems).*

**Copulation hat** — A rubber or silicone "turban" worn on the handler's head, textured with small dimples that collect semen when an imprint tiercel copulates on it. Invented by falconer Lester Boyd in the 1970s (first successful hatch 1975). *See [Breeding](/en/guide/breeding).*

***Coturnix*** **quail** — Captive-bred quail providing a near-ideal balanced protein, fat, calcium and vitamin profile for falcons; the closest available food to a "perfect" ration. *See [Nutrition](/en/guide/nutrition).*

**Courtship** — The pre-mating display sequence between a breeding pair: mutual food-passing, bowing at the ledge, shared ledge visits, soft vocalisations. *See [Breeding](/en/guide/breeding).*

**Coverts** — The feathers that cover the bases of the flight feathers. *See [Breeds & Types](/en/guide/breeds).*

**Creance** — A long lightweight training line (typically 15–30 m) attached to the bird's jesses via a swivel; allows controlled flights at progressively increasing distance without risk of losing the bird. *See [Training](/en/guide/training).*

**Crop** — The pouch in a bird's throat where food is initially stored before digestion. Should empty within 12–24 hours after a normal meal. *See [Daily Care](/en/guide/daily-care).*

**Crop impaction / Crop stasis / Sour crop** — Failure of the crop to empty, followed by bacterial and yeast overgrowth; the bird becomes systemically toxic within hours to days. A vet emergency; typically a sequel to spoiled or warm-thawed prey, dehydration, or underlying illness. *See [Health & Prevention](/en/guide/health).*

**Crusades** — The 11th–13th c. European military campaigns to the Levant that brought the polished Arab-Persian falconry tradition into direct contact with European courts. *See [History & Heritage](/en/guide/history).*

## D

**Daily room (غرفة الطيور)** — The year-round living space for a working falcon, where the bird is weighed, fed, observed, and rested outside training and weathering time. Not a cage — a purpose-built room. *See [Housing & Environment](/en/guide/housing).*

**Day-old chick (DOC)** — The widely-available staple prey: slightly low in calcium and slightly high in fat from residual yolk sac, so not ideal as sole food. *See [Nutrition](/en/guide/nutrition).*

***De Arte Venandi cum Avibus*** — "On the Art of Hunting with Birds"; the manuscript by Holy Roman Emperor Frederick II of Hohenstaufen, compiled over roughly thirty years and completed in the 1240s. The illuminated Vatican copy contains over 900 bird illustrations and is recognised as the first zoological treatise in the critical spirit of modern science. *See [History & Heritage](/en/guide/history).*

**DDT** — The pesticide whose agricultural use from the 1940s onwards caused catastrophic eggshell thinning and reproductive failure in peregrines and other raptors. Banned in the US in 1972; the trigger for the peregrine recovery effort. *See [History & Heritage](/en/guide/history).*

**Directional antenna** — The handheld antenna (typically a Yagi design with multiple prongs) used with a telemetry receiver to locate the strongest signal direction from the bird's transmitter. *See [Training](/en/guide/training).*

**Double-clutching** — Pulling the first clutch of eggs for artificial incubation to induce the female to lay a second clutch within the same season; common in commercial Gulf production. *See [Breeding](/en/guide/breeding).*

**Dropping** — The bird's excreta, with three clearly distinguishable parts in a healthy falcon: dark faecal portion, chalky-white urate portion, and a small clear liquid-urine portion. A key daily diagnostic tool. *See [Health & Prevention](/en/guide/health).*

**Duphalyte** — A combined fluid, electrolyte, B-vitamin, and amino-acid injectable solution used as supportive care for debilitated, dehydrated, or recovering birds. *See [Supplements & Vitamins](/en/guide/supplements).*

## E

**Egg-bound / Egg-binding** — Life-threatening failure of a female to pass a formed egg; visible as listlessness, straining, tail pumping, unwillingness to move. Calcium gluconate injection by an avian vet is the standard response. Often fatal if delayed. *See [Breeding](/en/guide/breeding).*

**Eggshell thinning** — The effect of DDT on raptor reproduction: shells became too thin to support embryo development, causing the mid-20th-century peregrine collapse. *See [History & Heritage](/en/guide/history).*

**Endoscopy** — A veterinary visualisation technique used to confirm aspergillosis in the lungs and air sacs. *See [Health & Prevention](/en/guide/health).*

**Entering to quarry** — The transition from training exercises (lure work, creance) to hunting live prey — when the bird makes the mental leap from "lure is the reward" to "live prey is the reward". *See [Training](/en/guide/training).*

**External parasites** — Lice, mites, and hippoboscid flies; treated with vet-prescribed permethrin, fipronil, or pyrethrin products at raptor-appropriate doses. Never use cat or dog products. *See [Health & Prevention](/en/guide/health).*

**Eyass** — A bird taken from the nest before fledging and hand-raised; imprints on the falconer, becomes very tame, but is also prone to screaming and other imprint-related behaviours. Most modern eyasses are captive-bred rather than wild-collected. *See [Breeds & Types](/en/guide/breeds).*

## F

**Faecal screen** — Testing of droppings for parasites and infection; recommended at quarantine start and end and routinely during the year. *See [Health & Prevention](/en/guide/health).*

**Falcon** (as female) — The term used in falconry for the **female** bird (the larger of a pair) — the one usually referred to simply as "the falcon". The male is the tiercel. *See [Breeds & Types](/en/guide/breeds).*

**Falcon herpesvirus** — A severe systemic viral disease, frequently fatal; transmitted from infected pigeons or infected falcons. A leading reason to never feed pigeon. *See [Health & Prevention](/en/guide/health).*

***Falcon Propagation*** — The Peregrine Fund's canonical manual on captive breeding, edited by Cade & Weaver. The worldwide reference for falcon AI and incubation. *See [Breeding](/en/guide/breeding).*

**Fallow year** — Deliberately skipping a breeding season to rest a proven pair and extend their total productive lifespan. *See [Breeding](/en/guide/breeding).*

**Fenbendazole** — An antiparasitic drug treating nematodes and tapeworms. *See [Supplements & Vitamins](/en/guide/supplements).*

**Fipronil** — An insecticide used at raptor-appropriate doses for external parasites. *See [Supplements & Vitamins](/en/guide/supplements).*

**Fledging / Fledge** — The first real flight from the nest or brooder; happens around days 35–45 depending on species (gyrfalcons slightly later). *See [Breeding](/en/guide/breeding).*

**Flight chamber** — The larger enclosed space where fledglings build wing strength after hatching or emerging from the brooder. *See [Breeding](/en/guide/breeding).*

**Flying weight** — The specific weight at which the bird is keen enough to hunt but heavy enough to stay in good condition. Individual to each bird and variable by species and season. *See [Nutrition](/en/guide/nutrition).*

**Footing** — To catch and hold prey (or the lure) with the talons. *See [Training](/en/guide/training).*

**Forced-air incubator** — The commercial-standard incubator design, circulating air to maintain temperature at 37.5 °C (99.5 °F) with very low drift. *See [Breeding](/en/guide/breeding).*

**Fostered clutch** — Eggs from one pair hatched under another pair; used when parents fail or to balance workload across a breeding programme. *See [Breeding](/en/guide/breeding).*

**Frederick II of Hohenstaufen** — (1194–1250) Holy Roman Emperor, king of Sicily, crusader; author of *De Arte Venandi cum Avibus*. Set the standard for direct observation over received authority in natural history. *See [History & Heritage](/en/guide/history).*

**Free flight** — Flying a bird without the creance. The single biggest judgement call in early training; requires fitted and tested telemetry. *See [Training](/en/guide/training).*

**Frounce (trichomoniasis)** — A protozoan infection caused by *Trichomonas gallinae*, transmitted through infected pigeons or doves. Diagnostic sign: yellowish "cheesy" lesions in the mouth or throat. Treatable with carnidazole (Spartrix) or metronidazole if caught early. *See [Health & Prevention](/en/guide/health).*

## G

**غرفة الطيور (Ghurfat al-Tuyur)** — The Arabic term for the **daily room** — a falcon's year-round housing. Distinct from the summer moulting chamber (**المقيض**). *See [Housing & Environment](/en/guide/housing).*

**Glove** — The heavy leather glove (long enough to protect wrist and forearm) on which a falcon perches during handling. Cleaned of blood and food after every session; conditioned periodically. *See [Daily Care](/en/guide/daily-care).*

**Gorge** — To feed heavily; in falconry, a full feed is sometimes called a "full gorge". *See [Nutrition](/en/guide/nutrition).*

**Ground-dwelling quarry** — Prey taken from the ground — houbara, stone curlew, hare — natural for sakers and traditional Bedouin falconry. *See [Training](/en/guide/training).*

**Gular flutter** — The throat flutter used for evaporative cooling; visible in heat-stressed or panting birds. *See [Health & Prevention](/en/guide/health).*

**Gulf falcon passport** — The international-movement documentation for Gulf-registered falcons; part of the modern infrastructure alongside captive-bred certification and microchipping. *See [History & Heritage](/en/guide/history).*

**Gyr × Saker hybrid** — The flagship Gulf hybrid, typically a gyrfalcon tiercel crossed with a saker female. Combines gyrfalcon size and power with saker heat tolerance and temperament. *See [Breeds & Types](/en/guide/breeds).*

**Gyrfalcon** — *Falco rusticolus*; the largest falcon in the world, a monotypic Arctic species historically known as "the bird of kings". *See [Breeds & Types](/en/guide/breeds).*

## H

**Haggard** — An adult wild-caught bird with a year or more of wild experience. Historically prized; illegal in most jurisdictions today. *See [Breeds & Types](/en/guide/breeds).*

**Hallux** — The inner hind toe of the foot; critical for gripping and for striking quarry. *See [Breeds & Types](/en/guide/breeds).*

**Hand-raised chick** — A chick removed from the nest and raised by the falconer in a brooder. Grows more slowly than parent-raised chicks and is prone to imprint-related behaviours. *See [Breeding](/en/guide/breeding).*

**Hatching window** — The 24–36 hour interval during which a clutch hatches, when incubation of all eggs was started together. *See [Breeding](/en/guide/breeding).*

**Hawk-of-station** — The medieval European social-ranking system assigning specific falcons to specific ranks: gyrfalcon for the king, peregrine for a prince or earl, saker for a knight, lanner for a squire, and so on down the hierarchy. More literary than operational — best known from the 15th-century *Boke of Saint Albans*. *See [History & Heritage](/en/guide/history).*

***hendersoni*** — *Falco cherrug hendersoni*, the high-elevation saker subspecies of Tibet and the Qinghai Plateau. *See [Breeds & Types](/en/guide/breeds).*

**Hood** — The leather hood that slides over a falcon's head; the primary transition-marker in training (hood-on = rest, hood-off = work). A well-fitted hood is transformative. *See [Daily Care](/en/guide/daily-care).*

**Houbara bustard** — The ground-dwelling game bird traditionally hunted with falcons in the Bedouin tradition; heavily regulated in modern times. *See [History & Heritage](/en/guide/history).*

**Houbara Fund** — The conservation programme that maintains the wild prey base on which working falconry depends, connecting the modern sport back to the ecological reality of its Bedouin origin. *See [History & Heritage](/en/guide/history).*

**Hybrid cross** — A deliberate cross between two species (e.g. gyr × saker). AI is effectively mandatory; cross-species pairs rarely bond naturally. *See [Breeding](/en/guide/breeding).*

## I

**Imprint / Imprint tiercel** — A bird hand-raised from within the first week after hatching, bonded on its human handler. **Imprint tiercels** are used for the copulation-hat AI method; they are a specialist multi-year investment and useful only for breeding, never for training or flying. *See [Breeding](/en/guide/breeding).*

**International Association for Falconry (IAF)** — The global federation coordinating falconry associations worldwide; works on legal, conservation, and cultural-heritage issues including the UNESCO inscription. *See [History & Heritage](/en/guide/history).*

**Itraconazole** — An antifungal drug used for treatment and prophylaxis of aspergillosis; requires vet supervision, is liver-toxic at high doses, and requires blood monitoring on long courses. *See [Supplements & Vitamins](/en/guide/supplements).*

## J

**Jesses** — The soft leather straps fastened to a falcon's legs; allow the falconer to control the bird without restraining it. Inspected daily for fraying. *See [Daily Care](/en/guide/daily-care).*

## K

**Keel** — The prominent breastbone, palpated during bird inspection. A sharp keel indicates an underweight bird; a well-muscled keel is the desired condition. *See [Breeds & Types](/en/guide/breeds).*

## L

**Lactated Ringer's solution** — A balanced electrolyte fluid for dehydration, shock, surgical support, or heat stroke; administered subcutaneously or intravenously under vet guidance. *See [Supplements & Vitamins](/en/guide/supplements).*

**Lead poisoning** — Toxicity from shot fragments in wild-caught prey or accidental ingestion of lead. Causes brown-stained urates, weakness, regurgitation, neurological signs, blindness, death. Requires vet chelation therapy. *See [Health & Prevention](/en/guide/health).*

**Leash** — The braided or flat cord connecting the swivel to a secure anchor point; long enough for the bird to move on its perch but not reach the ground or another bird. Checked daily for fraying — a failed leash is how birds get lost. *See [Daily Care](/en/guide/daily-care).*

**Lester Boyd** — The falconer from Pullman, Washington, who invented the copulation-hat artificial insemination technique in the 1970s; first successful hatch in 1975. His innovation is the technical foundation of all modern large-scale captive falcon breeding. *See [History & Heritage](/en/guide/history).*

**Lockdown** — The final 3 days of incubation, when egg turning stops and humidity rises to 65–70 % to soften the membrane for hatching. *See [Breeding](/en/guide/breeding).*

**Lure** — The leather pad with feathers or streamers on a cord, swung to simulate prey motion. The primary recall and conditioning tool for flown falcons, used across a bird's entire working life. *See [Training](/en/guide/training).*

## M

**Manning** — The calm, patient, repeated acclimation of a falcon to the falconer's hand, glove, voice, and daily presence. The foundation skill of early weeks; rushing it sets back everything that follows. *See [Daily Care](/en/guide/daily-care).*

**Mantling** — A food-protecting posture where the bird spreads its wings over food. Can indicate weight is too low; should not be reinforced by removing food in response. *See [Common Problems](/en/guide/problems).*

**المقيض (Al-Maqeez)** — The Arabic term for the **summer moulting chamber**; a separate, cooler, quieter space distinct from the daily room (غرفة الطيور). Used for roughly three months each summer during the moult. *See [Housing & Environment](/en/guide/housing).*

**Metronidazole** — An antiprotozoal drug used as an alternative to carnidazole for frounce treatment. *See [Supplements & Vitamins](/en/guide/supplements).*

**Mews** — The traditional falconry term for the falcon's living space; a purpose-built room (not a cage). The "daily room" in this guide's convention. *See [Housing & Environment](/en/guide/housing).*

**Microchip** — A permanent identification device injected under the skin, scannable by any vet. Mandatory in most jurisdictions for captive-bred certification; fitted from roughly day 21 onwards. *See [Breeding](/en/guide/breeding).*

***milvipes*** — *Falco cherrug milvipes*, the "mountain saker" subspecies widespread across Central Asia; the subspecies to which the Altai morph belongs. *See [Breeds & Types](/en/guide/breeds).*

**Ministry of Climate Change and Environment (UAE)** — The UAE authority that licenses and regulates falcon breeders. Counterpart authorities exist in Qatar, Saudi Arabia, Kuwait, Bahrain, and Oman. *See [Breeding](/en/guide/breeding).*

**Mongolian steppes** — The geographic heartland of ancient Central Asian falconry, one of the theorised cradles of the practice alongside Iran/Persia. *See [History & Heritage](/en/guide/history).*

**Morph** — A distinct colour and pattern variation within a species. Gyrfalcons show a continuous cline of morphs (white, silver, grey, dark). *See [Breeds & Types](/en/guide/breeds).*

**Moulting chamber (المقيض)** — See **المقيض**. *See [Housing & Environment](/en/guide/housing).*

**Mute** — Silent; a "mute" bird does not vocalise. *See [Daily Care](/en/guide/daily-care).*

## N

**Natural pair breeding** — The most traditional breeding method: a bonded pair in a dedicated chamber, with parent-raised chicks. Produces psychologically normal falcons suitable for training and flying. *See [Breeding](/en/guide/breeding).*

**Nesting ledge** — The specific structure in a breeding chamber where the female makes her scrape and lays her eggs. A shallow tray at shoulder height with a soft substrate (fine sand or pea gravel). *See [Breeding](/en/guide/breeding).*

**North American Falconers Association (NAFA)** — The regional falconry association for North America; a member of the IAF. *See [History & Heritage](/en/guide/history).*

## O

**Obligate carnivore** — An animal that must digest whole vertebrate prey and cannot thrive on muscle meat alone. Falcons are obligate carnivores and need whole prey for bone, organ, and roughage content. *See [Nutrition](/en/guide/nutrition).*

## P

**Parallel evolution hypothesis** — The theory that falconry arose independently in the Mongolian steppes and in Iran/Persia at roughly the same ancient period, then spread outward — reconciling two ancient traditions without forcing a single point of origin. *See [History & Heritage](/en/guide/history).*

**Parent-raised chick** — A chick raised naturally by its parents; develops normal species-recognition and becomes a psychologically normal falcon. The preferred method for producing birds intended for training and flying. *See [Breeding](/en/guide/breeding).*

**Passage** — A juvenile falcon caught during its first autumn migration; has wild hunting instincts but has not yet learned to avoid humans. Historically the traditional working bird of falconry; today legal only in specific jurisdictions and seasons. *See [Breeds & Types](/en/guide/breeds).*

**Peregrine** — *Falco peregrinus*; the speed specialist of falconry, the fastest animal in a stoop (over 320 km/h measured), and a bird specialist in the wild. The European royal falcon in historical terms and the focus of modern conservation. *See [Breeds & Types](/en/guide/breeds).*

***peregrinator*** — *Falco peregrinus peregrinator*, the black shaheen; a resident peregrine subspecies across South Asia from the Indian subcontinent to Sri Lanka. *See [Breeds & Types](/en/guide/breeds).*

**Peregrine collapse** — The mid-20th-century near-extinction of peregrines across North America and parts of Europe caused by DDT-induced eggshell thinning. *See [History & Heritage](/en/guide/history).*

**Peregrine Fund, The** — Founded in 1970 at Cornell University; the institutional heart of the peregrine recovery programme and the publisher of Cade & Weaver's *Falcon Propagation* manual. *See [History & Heritage](/en/guide/history).*

***peregrinus*** — *Falco peregrinus peregrinus*, the nominate Eurasian peregrine subspecies. *See [Breeds & Types](/en/guide/breeds).*

**Permethrin** — An insecticide used at raptor-appropriate doses for external parasites. Never use cat or dog products — concentrations differ. *See [Supplements & Vitamins](/en/guide/supplements).*

**Pip / Pipping** — The first visible crack in the shell made by the chick's egg tooth. "External pip" is the visible crack; it typically precedes full hatch by 18–24 hours. *See [Breeding](/en/guide/breeding).*

**Pitch** — The height at which a bird "waits on" above the falconer or anticipated quarry. Higher pitch produces more dramatic stoops and greater momentum. *See [Training](/en/guide/training).*

**Plumage** — The bird's feather coat; completeness, condition, and tidiness are key health and condition indicators. *See [Daily Care](/en/guide/daily-care).*

**Pododermatitis** — The medical term for bumblefoot. *See [Health & Prevention](/en/guide/health).*

**Praziquantel** — An antiparasitic drug treating tapeworms and trematodes. *See [Supplements & Vitamins](/en/guide/supplements).*

**President's Cup** — The flagship falcon-racing event, held in Abu Dhabi; one of the anchor events of modern competitive Gulf falconry. *See [History & Heritage](/en/guide/history).*

**Primaries** — The outermost flight feathers of the wing; counted for plumage completeness during bird inspection. *See [Breeds & Types](/en/guide/breeds).*

**Prolapse** — See **Cloacal prolapse**.

**Prophylaxis / Prophylactic** — Preventive medical treatment given before clinical signs appear; e.g. antifungal prophylaxis for a high-risk gyrfalcon entering Gulf summer. *See [Supplements & Vitamins](/en/guide/supplements).*

**Proven pair** — A breeding pair that has produced at least one fertile clutch. Proven pairs command substantial premiums over unproven ones. *See [Breeding](/en/guide/breeding).*

## Q

**Quarantine** — Isolating a new bird for at least 30 days in separate air space before any contact with resident birds. Includes faecal screens at start and end and a full vet check before integration. The single most important biosecurity practice in multi-bird keeping. *See [Housing & Environment](/en/guide/housing).*

**Quarry** — Game pursued in hunting; in Gulf falconry traditionally houbara bustard, stone curlew, and hare for sakers, and flighted game (ducks, pigeons) for peregrines. *See [Training](/en/guide/training).*

## R

**Ration** — The daily food portion. Rough guidelines by species: saker 8–12 % of body weight, peregrine 10–15 %, gyrfalcon 6–10 %. Varies with season, training intensity, and individual bird. *See [Nutrition](/en/guide/nutrition).*

**Recall** — The bird's return to the falconer or the lure; the single most fundamental trained behaviour. *See [Training](/en/guide/training).*

**Receiver** (telemetry) — The handheld unit that detects signal strength and direction from the transmitter attached to the bird. *See [Training](/en/guide/training).*

**Resting weight** — The higher weight a falcon carries during the off-season and pre-moult periods, when not being flown or trained. *See [Nutrition](/en/guide/nutrition).*

**Ring / Ringing** — Fitting of a closed leg ring to a chick while the tarsus is still small enough (day 10–14). Required for captive-bred certification. Not to be confused with "ring up" (altitude-building flight behaviour). *See [Breeding](/en/guide/breeding).*

**Ring up** — To gain altitude in circles; the characteristic flight behaviour that builds pitch before a stoop. *See [Training](/en/guide/training).*

**Roughage** — Indigestible material (feather, fur, small bone fragments) needed for the natural casting cycle. Obtained from whole prey. *See [Nutrition](/en/guide/nutrition).*

**Rouse** — A calm shake-out of feathers; normal, relaxed behaviour, not to be confused with mantling or distress. *See [Daily Care](/en/guide/daily-care).*

***rusticolus*** — The specific epithet of the gyrfalcon, *Falco rusticolus*. *See [Breeds & Types](/en/guide/breeds).*

## S

**Saker** — *Falco cherrug*; the historical working falcon of the Central Asian steppes and the Bedouin desert. A generalist hunter taking ground mammals and birds in the wild; the most forgiving of the three species to keep in Gulf conditions. *See [Breeds & Types](/en/guide/breeds).*

**Saker × Peregrine hybrid** — An intermediate hybrid, less common than either gyr hybrid; sometimes called "perlin" or "sakeret" informally. *See [Breeds & Types](/en/guide/breeds).*

**Scale** — The gram-accurate digital scale used for daily weighing. Same scale, same time, before food — every day. *See [Nutrition](/en/guide/nutrition).*

**Scrape** — The shallow depression the female makes in the nesting-ledge substrate; where the eggs are laid. *See [Breeding](/en/guide/breeding).*

**Screaming** — An imprint-related attention-seeking vocalisation learned by hand-raised chicks when vocalisations bring food or contact. Often permanent once established; reinforced by any response while screaming. *See [Common Problems](/en/guide/problems).*

**Secondaries** — The inner flight feathers of the wing; checked for completeness during bird inspection. *See [Breeds & Types](/en/guide/breeds).*

**Semen collection** — Gathered from an imprint tiercel on the copulation hat during the breeding season; peak collection from an individual tiercel is roughly every 2–3 days. *See [Breeding](/en/guide/breeding).*

**Sheikh Zayed bin Sultan Al Nahyan** — (1918–2004) founding president of the United Arab Emirates; one of the most consequential figures in modernising Gulf falconry through breeding programmes, hospitals, and the international-diplomacy dimension that produced the UNESCO inscription. *See [History & Heritage](/en/guide/history).*

**Shelf perch** — A padded shelf mounted on the wall at bird height; gives variety of position alongside a block perch. *See [Housing & Environment](/en/guide/housing).*

**Silk Road** — The ancient trade and migration corridors along which falconry moved from Central Asia west to Persia and the Levant, east to China and Korea, south into the Arabian Peninsula. UNESCO recognises falconry as one of the defining living heritages of the trade-route world. *See [History & Heritage](/en/guide/history).*

**Silver morph** — A pale grey-toned gyrfalcon colour morph; at a distance can resemble an oversized lanner falcon. *See [Breeds & Types](/en/guide/breeds).*

**Soliciting** — The female's pre-copulation invitation behaviour at the nesting ledge; a key pre-lay sign. *See [Breeding](/en/guide/breeding).*

**Souq Waqif Falcon Hospital** — The Doha-based falcon hospital; one of the three anchor institutions of Gulf falcon medicine. *See [History & Heritage](/en/guide/history).*

**Sour crop** — See **Crop impaction**.

**Stamina test** — A resting-vs-post-exercise breathing-recovery assessment; used to diagnose the severity of aspergillosis and other respiratory conditions. *See [Health & Prevention](/en/guide/health).*

**Step-up** — The foundation training action: the bird steps from a perch or scale onto the gloved fist for food. The first "action" under active training. *See [Training](/en/guide/training).*

**Still-air incubator** — An incubator without forced air circulation; runs slightly higher at the shell top (37.8–38.3 °C) due to air stratification. Less common than forced-air in commercial use. *See [Breeding](/en/guide/breeding).*

**Stone curlew** — A game bird traditionally hunted with falcons in the Bedouin tradition. *See [Training](/en/guide/training).*

**Stoop / Stooping** — The high-speed diving attack from altitude; the classic falcon hunting mode. Peregrines achieve measured speeds over 320 km/h. *See [Training](/en/guide/training).*

**Styptic powder** — A bleed-stopping powder used for broken blood feathers or bleeding talons; standard in a falconer's first-aid kit. *See [Health & Prevention](/en/guide/health).*

**Subspecies** — A geographically or morphologically distinct population within a species. Sakers have four recognised subspecies (*cherrug*, *milvipes*, *coatsi*, *hendersoni*); peregrines about 19 worldwide; gyrfalcons are monotypic (no subspecies). *See [Breeds & Types](/en/guide/breeds).*

**Surgical AI** — Depositing semen directly into the female's oviduct under anaesthesia; a specialist veterinary technique used in specific programmes where cloacal AI has been unreliable. *See [Breeding](/en/guide/breeding).*

**Swivel** — The small brass or steel swivel between jesses and leash that prevents the leash from twisting. Must spin freely and is inspected daily for seizure. *See [Daily Care](/en/guide/daily-care).*

## T

**Talon** — A falcon's claw; can overgrow and require careful trimming. *See [Common Problems](/en/guide/problems).*

**Tang Dynasty** — 7th–10th c. AD Chinese dynasty during which falconry was established as an imperial sport, marking falconry's eastern Silk Road reach. *See [History & Heritage](/en/guide/history).*

**Tarsus** — The lower-leg segment of a bird; the site where closed leg rings are fitted during days 10–14 of chick life. *See [Breeding](/en/guide/breeding).*

**Telemetry** — The radio-tracking system (transmitter, receiver, directional antenna) that allows a falconer to locate a bird that is out of sight. **Every falconer flying a bird free must understand telemetry before the first free flight.** Not optional. *See [Training](/en/guide/training).*

**Tiercel** — The male falcon, from Latin *tertius* ("a third"), because he is approximately one-third smaller than the female. *See [Breeds & Types](/en/guide/breeds).*

**Toltrazuril** — The standard drug treating coccidiosis. *See [Supplements & Vitamins](/en/guide/supplements).*

**Transmitter** (telemetry) — The small radio unit attached to the bird (usually on a leg or tail-mount) that emits the continuous signal the receiver locates. Battery must be fresh before every flight. *See [Training](/en/guide/training).*

**Treading** — The male's mounting behaviour during copulation. *See [Breeding](/en/guide/breeding).*

***Trichomonas gallinae*** — The protozoan parasite that causes frounce; transmitted via pigeon contact. The single best reason to never feed pigeon. *See [Nutrition](/en/guide/nutrition).*

**Trichomoniasis** — See **Frounce**.

**Turning** (incubation) — The routine rotation of eggs during incubation — minimum 3 times daily, ideally every 1–2 hours in automatic incubators. Turning stops at lockdown (last 3 days). *See [Breeding](/en/guide/breeding).*

## U

**UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage** — The international designation under which falconry is inscribed on the UNESCO Representative List of the Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity. Inscribed in 2010 with 11 founding countries led by the UAE; extended in 2012, 2016, and 2021 to a current total of **24 countries** — the largest multi-national element on the list. *See [History & Heritage](/en/guide/history).*

**Unproven pair** — A breeding pair that has not yet produced a fertile clutch. *See [Breeding](/en/guide/breeding).*

**Unzipping** — The chick's rotation and chipping of a spiral line around the blunt end of the shell as the final stage of hatching. *See [Breeding](/en/guide/breeding).*

**Urates** — The chalky-white portion of a bird's dropping; colour changes (yellow, green, brown) can indicate organ problems or lead poisoning. *See [Health & Prevention](/en/guide/health).*

## V

**Vent** — The anal opening; the area around it should be clean and dry, not soiled. *See [Daily Care](/en/guide/daily-care).*

**Veterinary certificate** — A certificate from an avian vet (captive-bred certification, health certificate for transport) required for any legitimate transfer of a falcon. *See [Breeding](/en/guide/breeding).*

**Vitamin AD3E injectable** — A high-concentration injectable form of fat-soluble vitamins A, D3, and E; used for diagnosed deficiency or severe illness recovery under vet guidance. *See [Supplements & Vitamins](/en/guide/supplements).*

**Voriconazole** — An antifungal drug used as an alternative to itraconazole in aspergillosis treatment. *See [Supplements & Vitamins](/en/guide/supplements).*

## W

**Wait on** — The high-altitude hunting behaviour where the bird circles above the falconer or anticipated quarry, maintaining altitude and readiness to stoop. *See [Training](/en/guide/training).*

**Warble** — The soft vocalisation made during courtship or in a calm state. *See [Daily Care](/en/guide/daily-care).*

**Weathering** — Outdoor time on a block or bow perch; sun, fresh air, exercise, mental stimulation, and natural temperature regulation. Essential for bird condition. *See [Daily Care](/en/guide/daily-care).*

**Weathering yard** — The outdoor perching area bridging the daily room and the training field; shaded in Gulf summer, never used at midday in the hottest months. *See [Housing & Environment](/en/guide/housing).*

**Weight management** — Daily gram-accurate weighing and ration adjustment to maintain the bird's flying weight; trends over time matter more than single-day readings. *See [Nutrition](/en/guide/nutrition).*

**White morph** — The most prized gyrfalcon colour morph, concentrated in Greenland: nearly pure white with light markings. Historically a diplomatic gift between rulers; the highest-value birds in the Gulf market today. *See [Breeds & Types](/en/guide/breeds).*

**Whole prey** — The non-negotiable nutritional foundation: muscle, organs, bone, and feather/fur for roughage. Muscle meat alone lacks the calcium, organ vitamins, and roughage a falcon needs. *See [Nutrition](/en/guide/nutrition).*

## Species-specific vocabulary

Each species has its own distinct terminology — subspecies, colour morphs, market vocabulary — worth gathering in one place. Switch the tabs above to see your species.

<SpeciesOnly species="saker">
### Saker — species-specific vocabulary

- **Subspecies** — four recognised: ***cherrug*** (nominate steppe saker), ***milvipes*** (mountain saker — the most widespread and the subspecies of the Altai morph), ***coatsi*** (desert saker of Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan), ***hendersoni*** (high-elevation saker of Tibet and the Qinghai Plateau).
- **Altai falcon / Altai morph** — a darker, heavily-spotted colour morph within *milvipes*; not a separate species or hybrid, despite historical debate, confirmed by a 2023 genetic study.
- **Arabic market vocabulary** — regional Gulf names for colour phases include **red saker**, **black saker**, and **chocolate**. These are market labels, not scientific taxonomy, and vary between emirates and tribes.
- **الحرّ (al-hurr)** — "the free one"; the Arabic name for the saker. *See the main glossary entry for context.*
- **Female saker** vs **saker tiercel** — females are the working standard in Gulf falconry; tiercels at the tier of serious work are uncommon.
</SpeciesOnly>

<SpeciesOnly species="peregrine">
### Peregrine — species-specific vocabulary

- **Subspecies** — about 19 globally; six commonly encountered: ***peregrinus*** (nominate Eurasian), ***anatum*** (American "duck hawk"), ***calidus*** (tundra peregrine, traditionally the Gulf passage bird), ***babylonicus*** (red-naped shaheen), ***peregrinator*** (black shaheen), ***brookei*** (Mediterranean peregrine / "Maltese falcon").
- **Shaheen naming note** — the Arabic word **الشاهين** is traditionally applied to *calidus* in the Gulf but also to *babylonicus* and *peregrinator* elsewhere in the wider Arabic and South Asian world. Modern captive-bred peregrines are often of mixed-subspecies lineage, so the naming question is often academic.
- **Bird-specialist** — peregrines are natural bird-catchers in the wild (ducks, pigeons, pheasants) and translate this directly to high-pitch lure work and stoops.
- **Peregrine Fund, The** — the 1970-founded institution at Cornell University that led the DDT-era recovery of this species and publishes *Falcon Propagation*.
</SpeciesOnly>

<SpeciesOnly species="gyrfalcon">
### Gyrfalcon — species-specific vocabulary

- **Monotypic** — the gyrfalcon has **no recognised subspecies**; colour variation within the species is along a continuous cline, not in discrete subspecies categories.
- **Colour morphs** — **white morph** (concentrated in Greenland; the most prized; historically a diplomatic gift between rulers), **silver morph** (pale grey), **grey morph** (the most common globally), **dark/black morph** (more common in northern Canada). Individual birds often sit between categories on the cline.
- **الجير (al-jeer)** — "the northern visitor"; the Arabic name for the gyrfalcon.
- **Bird of kings** — the gyr's historical status in medieval European hawk-of-station tables (king only) and as a diplomatic gift in the Mongol and Arab traditions. The cultural weight persists in the Gulf market today.
- **CITES Appendix I** — in many jurisdictions, the highest protection tier; captive-bred documentation for gyrfalcons is especially consequential.
</SpeciesOnly>
